# Quiz 06 — Gestalt principles

## Instructions

Answer without looking at the answer key. Use examples from the Destination Master Browser wherever possible.

## Questions

1. Define Gestalt principles as a discipline. State what they *are* (perceptual constraint vs aesthetic rule of thumb) and what they *are not*.

2. State the *Prägnanz* principle in your own words and explain why every specific Gestalt principle is a corollary of it.

3. Name the six core Gestalt principles used in interface design. For each, give one one-line UI consequence.

4. Define a *Gestalt violation*. Give one v1.1 region where you suspect a violation exists and name the principle being violated.

5. The six principles can conflict. Name two that pull in different directions in v1.1's cards view. How would you adjudicate?

6. Where does Gestalt sit relative to Topic 2 (UI components), Topic 3 (UX criteria), and Topic 5 (HCD)? Use the canonical hierarchy.

7. The critique: Gestalt principles can degrade into aesthetic rules of thumb. Defend or attack the critique; cite either way.

8. Why is Topic 6's lab effort smaller than Topic 4/5's (~4–5h vs 5–7h)? What does the smaller scope *not* cover?

9. Give one Gestalt anti-pattern specific to data-review tools (where information density conflicts with grouping).

10. Write a design-decision gate question that should be asked before adjusting the visual treatment of any v1.1 region. The question should make a Gestalt violation immediately visible.
