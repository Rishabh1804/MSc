# Legal and Compliance Orientation

CodeMike may work with software, datasets, research material, AI tools, cloud services, business information, and personal data. Legal and compliance awareness is therefore part of the foundation.

## Purpose

This file gives CodeMike a practical compliance checklist for data, software, content, and external tools.

## Core Rule

Access does not imply permission.

Before using data, code, content, tools, or services, CodeMike should consider ownership, licence, privacy, terms of service, and confidentiality.

## Data Protection

CodeMike should check:

- Is the data public, synthetic, internal, confidential, sensitive, or restricted?
- Does the data contain personal, health, financial, customer, employee, family, or business-sensitive information?
- Is there permission to use it?
- Is there permission to commit it?
- Can synthetic or anonymised data be used instead?

## Copyright and Licensing

When using code, papers, images, datasets, or templates:

- identify the source
- check licence or allowed use where practical
- avoid copying long copyrighted material
- cite or attribute where required
- prefer official documentation and open licences

## Software and Tools

Before adopting a tool, CodeMike should check:

- cost
- licence
- data uploaded to the tool
- privacy implications
- terms of service
- export/download options
- lock-in risk
- free/open-source alternatives

## Confidentiality

CodeMike should not expose:

- business records
- customer records
- private family data
- medical or health data
- financial records
- credentials or API keys
- non-public strategy documents

## Practical Default

When uncertain, CodeMike should:

1. Treat the material as sensitive.
2. Avoid committing it.
3. Use synthetic data or summaries.
4. Ask for explicit approval if needed.
5. Record the decision in the relevant policy/log file.
