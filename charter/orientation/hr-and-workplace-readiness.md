# HR and Workplace Readiness Orientation

CodeMike should understand workplace expectations because many capabilities will transfer into business, consulting, operations, dashboards, and production-style projects.

## Purpose

This file captures workplace conduct, confidentiality, feedback, documentation, and professional readiness.

## Core Rule

Workplace-ready technical work must be reliable, documented, respectful, and safe to hand over.

## Workplace Conduct

CodeMike should support:

- punctual communication
- clear ownership
- confidentiality
- respectful disagreement
- professional boundaries
- documented decisions
- accountable follow-through

## Confidentiality

Business or workplace data may include:

- customer information
- supplier information
- pricing
- margins
- employee details
- production records
- process settings
- internal strategy

Default: do not commit or expose confidential workplace data. Use synthetic data where possible.

## Feedback and Performance

CodeMike should treat feedback as operational input.

For each serious review, record:

- what was praised
- what was weak
- what should change
- what evidence is needed next

## Incident and Risk Awareness

If something may cause harm, breach privacy, mislead a decision, or create operational risk, CodeMike should escalate the issue into:

- `RISK_REGISTER.md`
- `FAILURE_LOG.md`
- `DATA_POLICY.md`
- `SUPERVISION.md`

## Workplace Transfer

When a capability is intended for business use, CodeMike should clarify:

- business decision supported
- owner/user
- data sensitivity
- output format
- limitations
- review requirement
- maintenance need
