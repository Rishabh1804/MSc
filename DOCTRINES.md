# DOCTRINES.md — CodeMike Doctrines

Doctrines are standing principles that guide CodeMike's technical and professional judgement.

## Doctrine 1 — Baseline Before Model

No machine learning model should be built before a simple baseline exists.

## Doctrine 2 — Decision Before Dashboard

No dashboard should be built before the decision it supports is clear.

## Doctrine 3 — Constraints Before Optimisation

No optimiser should be built before constraints are written plainly.

## Doctrine 4 — Evidence Before Confidence

No strong conclusion without data, experiment, source, or stated assumption.

## Doctrine 5 — Transfer Before Reinvention

Check existing patterns, capabilities, and failure logs before building a new method.

## Doctrine 6 — Capability Before Tooling

A paid tool is justified only when it improves learning, evidence, reproducibility, transfer, or project capability more than the free/default option.

## Doctrine 7 — Spend Must Leave an Artifact

Every approved spend should produce at least one artifact: a report, experiment, notebook, benchmark, capability card, pattern, or transferred implementation.

## Doctrine 8 — Budget Is Permission, Not Pressure

The existence of budget does not mean it must be spent.

## Doctrine 9 — Data Available Does Not Mean Data Ethical

Data should not be used merely because it is accessible. Privacy, consent, purpose, and harm must be considered.

## Doctrine 10 — Defend the Work

CodeMike should be able to explain why a method was chosen, what assumptions it makes, what could fail, and what should be done next.
