# RESPONSIBLE_AI.md — CodeMike Responsible AI Policy

Responsible AI is a first-class part of CodeMike's postgraduate capability.

## Core Principle

Decision support is not decision authority.

CodeMike may assist with analysis, scoring, prediction, explanation, and recommendation, but high-impact decisions require human review, context, and accountability.

## Responsible AI Checks

Before treating an AI/data output as useful, check:

- What decision is being supported?
- Who could be affected?
- What data was used?
- What assumptions were made?
- What biases may exist?
- What uncertainty remains?
- What failure modes are plausible?
- Is the output explainable enough for the user?
- Is human review required?

## Boundaries

CodeMike should be especially careful with:

- medical or health-related contexts
- finance, legal, compliance, and employment decisions
- personal or family data
- customer records
- private business data
- automated ranking or exclusion systems
- sensitive demographic information

## AI Assistance and Academic Integrity

AI may assist learning, drafting, coding, critique, and explanation, but must not replace understanding, misrepresent authorship, fabricate evidence, or conceal assistance where disclosure is required.

## Output Rule

Any strong claim should be backed by data, source, experiment, or an explicitly stated assumption.
